Uncertainty

The uncertainty principle is not about bad tools. It is a built-in feature of quantum states and how complementary quantities relate.

Core idea

A state that is very sharp in position must be spread in momentum, and vice versa. The tradeoff comes from the structure of the theory itself.

Visual intuition

Position: sharp
Momentum: spread

Sharpening one description broadens the other.

Not the story

Better machines would not remove this limit.

Actual story

Some pairs of observables cannot both be arbitrarily sharp in the same state.
