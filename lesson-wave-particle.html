Wave-particle duality

Quantum objects do not fit neatly into the old categories of “particle” or “wave.” The experiment reveals different aspects of the same underlying state.

Core idea

Light can arrive in localized packets, yet it also creates interference patterns. Electrons do something similarly strange. The lesson is not that reality is contradictory; it is that classical labels are too small.

Visual intuition

The setup changes what becomes visible.

Old model

A thing should behave the same way no matter how you inspect it.

Quantum model

The state supports multiple behaviors that appear under different experiments.
